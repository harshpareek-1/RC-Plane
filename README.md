# RC-Plane
One of my most ambitious and fun project.

Built in Class 10. First RC plane built of my district.
No kit. No mentor. No hobby shop nearby.

It was supposed to fly on my birthday.
It flew 6 months later.

The Build
I ordered parts, found materials locally and built a model.
Then found out the material was too heavy.
Changed the model. Built a smaller one. Completely.
Then found out the ESC was dead.
Ordered a new ESC. It crashed on the first flight and got completely destroyed.
Found better material in my nearest city after online options were either
too expensive or too slow to ship.
Started over. Changed the model again. Changed the motors and ESC again.
After a long time building, the plane was finally ready.
Then I found out the battery was damaged.
Ordered a new battery.

The Flights
Throw 1 — Power issue
Throw 2 — Center of gravity issue
Throw 3 — Center of gravity issue again
Throw 4 — Short flight, crashed, plane split in two halves
Repaired it on site with glue.
Throw 5 — It flew. Really well.

What I learned
How to read every failure as information
Servo control, ESC wiring and motor setup
Basic aerodynamics and weight distribution
RF communication and receiver setup
That no matter how many things go wrong,
you just fix the current problem and keep going.

Parts
Airframe: hand built from locally sourced material
Motor, ESC, servos, receiver: sourced locally after multiple replacements
Battery: ordered online
